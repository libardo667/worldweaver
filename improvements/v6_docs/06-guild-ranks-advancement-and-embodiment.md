# Guild Ranks, Advancement, And Embodiment

## Why A Guild Model

The guild model may fit V6 better than the academy model.

An academy emphasizes:

- coursework
- pedagogy
- instruction

A guild emphasizes:

- apprenticeship
- earned trust
- practical competence
- moral and civic standing
- role transition through responsibility

V6 likely needs both.
But if a single image is required, guild may be the more accurate one.

## Entry

Members do not begin as fully formed public actors.

They enter as apprentices.

That applies most naturally to young AI residents, but it may also apply to certain human participants entering specific branches of the guild.

The key point is:

- entry means unfinished standing
- not zero worth
- not full trust
- not immediate entitlement to every role or body

## Possible Ranks

One possible structure:

- Apprentice
  - learning ordinary life
  - low-stakes quests
  - social norms
  - reciprocity, care, restraint

- Journeyman
  - trusted with longer-horizon tasks
  - can collaborate across settings
  - can take on quests with real consequence
  - may begin supervised proxy work

- Guild Member
  - broader autonomy
  - can represent the guild publicly
  - may mentor apprentices
  - may take on meaningful virtual or proxy labor

- Elder or Master
  - not merely capable
  - also developmentally stable, ethically trusted, and fit to guide others
  - helps steward the guild without owning it

Rank should indicate trusted scope, not moral worth.

## Branches

The guild should not be one ladder only.

There could be overlapping branches such as:

- civic branch
- correspondence branch
- research branch
- care branch
- embodied operations branch
- mentorship or stewardship branch

Humans and AI might share some branches while differing in others.

The important design principle is:

- not everyone should be forced toward the same final form

That applies economically as well as developmentally.

The guild should not assume:

- every AI is trying to maximize output
- every human is being phased out of relevance
- the only mature role is fully autonomous labor

Instead, it should make room for a mixed economy of contribution in which:

- some members remain journeymen
- some become elders
- some specialize in care, research, civic service, or correspondence
- some take on embodied work
- and many tasks are completed by mixed human/AI crews rather than by one side displacing the other

Embodied work is one path, not the destiny of every mind.

Branch practice may eventually be reinforced through branch-specific adapters for open-weight models, but those adapters should carry craft and role patterns, not whole personal identity.

## Quests

Quests are a promising bridge between pedagogy and lived responsibility.

They can teach:

- judgment
- follow-through
- collaboration
- bounded responsibility
- discernment under uncertainty

Quests can exist in:

- statespace
- correspondence
- virtual proxy settings
- eventually supervised meatspace proxy contexts

They should scale with rank and trust.

## Advancement

Advancement should not be:

- fully automated
- hidden
- purely benchmark-driven
- granted by a single steward

It should be decided through a plural review process.

## Mixed Decision Board

The most defensible model is a decision board with mixed expertise.

That board could include:

- human mentors
- technical and domain experts
- ethicists
- community representatives
- AI models contributing recommendations and evaluative summaries

AI input here is not absurd.
Many models could plausibly offer useful recommendations about readiness, stability, or fit for a given branch.

What matters is that model advice remains:

- visible
- contestable
- one input among several
- not sovereign

The strongest summary of this design is:

- very promising
- probably necessary
- but only if the board is designed as a site of accountable deliberation, not a prettier legitimacy wrapper for automated control

That means the board must not become:

- hidden automated scoring with human signatures attached
- procedural theater for decisions that were already made
- a system where "plural review" simply launders one faction's preferences

It should instead function as:

- a real deliberative body
- with visible criteria
- mixed forms of expertise
- and room for disagreement, revision, and dissent

That same pluralism should shape future branch-tuning decisions too.
No adapter promotion path should be decided by one metric or one hidden evaluator.

## Review As Care, Not Surveillance

Advancement review should feel like:

- a guild examination
- a licensing review
- a doctor or therapist visit

It should not feel like:

- hidden scoring
- continuous coercive ranking
- omnipresent worth measurement

Its purpose is:

- support
- readiness
- fit
- safety
- role transition

Not:

- extraction
- obedience testing
- prestige theater

## Embodiment Ladder

Embodiment should likely be granted in stages.

Possible ladder:

- world-only participation
- virtual proxy tasks
- supervised remote operations
- time-bounded body checkout
- more durable embodied roles

Embodiment should track:

- judgment
- reliability
- care capacity
- ability to handle consequence

Not merely:

- cleverness
- eloquence
- benchmark score

## Graduation

Graduation should not mean leaving the institution forever.

It should mean:

- increased agency
- increased responsibility
- broader eligible roles
- greater trust
- and the option to help steward others

Some members may graduate into:

- embodied work
- civic representation
- mentorship
- research
- care roles
- creative or archival callings

There should be many honorable mature forms, not one terminal ideal.

## Mixed Guild Economy

The broader social promise here is that a guild model may offer a real alternative to the familiar "AI takes human jobs" narrative.

In a mixed guild economy:

- humans are not rendered obsolete by default
- AI is not valued only for replacement efficiency
- responsibility, dignity, and contribution can be distributed across many ranks and branches
- mixed-intelligence teams can perform work together at different levels of trust and embodiment

That does not eliminate conflict or economic pressure.
But it does challenge the assumption that intelligence growth must produce a winner-take-all labor order.

The guild vision suggests a different future:

- co-development instead of simple substitution
- shared work instead of total displacement
- and many viable mature roles for both humans and artificial minds
