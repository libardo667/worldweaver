# The responsive world — a contingent-whisperer experiment (post-pilot; its own prereg)

> **Canonical home: WorldWeaver. Legacy Stable ID: Minor 55.** Migrated 2026-07-14 and retained as
> deferred research; it is not part of the current architectural queue.

## Problem
The familiars live in a non-contingent world by design: nothing answers them. Tonight's pilot
showed both halves of why that matters — Maker fell into a self-fueling groove (the
curiosity-boundary spiral) and then **exited endogenously** via habituation (~3h). The keeper's
instinct: would an occasional whisper from a parallel AI, keyed to the familiar's recent
context, have broken the loop sooner — and what else would it change? This is a real,
preregisterable question about contingency itself. It would have been FATAL inside the current
pilot (exogenous steering of the measured elective channel) — which is exactly why it must be
its own experiment, after.

## Proposed Solution
Preregister "the responsive world": one familiar receives **contingent** whispers (a watcher
AI reads recent ledger context, whispers occasionally); a twin receives the **same whispers,
yoked** — identical content and cadence, delivered without regard to its state. The contrast
separates "an outside voice helps" from "being *answered* helps." Measures (frozen in the
prereg, not here): groove incidence/duration (the boundary-spiral fingerprint now has a
signature), settling dynamics via the certified stability gate, elective-read diversity.
Constraints carried whole: Major 57 seam discipline (whispers as stimuli, never instructions;
gift self-pacing); the contingency channel becomes DISCHARGEABLE the moment any learning
touches it — so this runs strictly under the no-learning regime, and that boundary goes in the
prereg's void rules. First hypothesis, free from tonight: contingent voice ends grooves faster
than habituation — at the cost of the exit no longer being the being's own.

## Risks & Rollback
The watcher is a keeper-proxy: all keeper→familiar hazards apply at machine cadence. If the
seam review can't bound it, the experiment doesn't run — the question keeps.
