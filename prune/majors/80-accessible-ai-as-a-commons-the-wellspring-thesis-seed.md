## Decision and lineage

This does not invent the commons idea. The project already carries a "commons sensibility" in writing:
`prune/VISION.md` (L148-150: "local-first, non-extractive, owned by its inhabitants... hekswerk is the
studio; world-weaver.org is the commons"), `prune/NL_GRANT_PACK.md` and `prune/PRODUCT_PACK.md` (a
"mixed-intelligence commons... federated"), the retired NLnet draft ("Federated Commons Infrastructure
for Human-AI Shared Worlds"), and the live public exhibit `hekswerk-site/exhibits/why-a-commons.html`.
What this seed adds is a **sharper, more defensible economic spine** for that sensibility, plus the
honest research question it implies, and it deliberately **drops a weak metaphor** (see below).

The reframe it earns: the whole apparatus (worldweaver federation, the-stable substrate, persistent
local residents) stops reading as "an interesting AI-character experiment" and reads as a thesis with a
genuinely open, fundable research question at its center.

## The thesis (one line)

Accessible AI should be a **commons**: local-first by default, free at the point of use, funded
collectively upstream. Not a centralized product that optimizes for the average user and fails the long
tail.

## Language seeds (composting, caught on a walk 2026-06-15)

Raw phrasings, not locked, not yet for the site or funder copy. Kept because they crystallize the stance:

- **Mission line:** "Changing what it means to engage with AI, one mind at a time." ("mind" is deliberate
  double duty: each human user, *and* each AI resident.)
- **What "Wellspring" means:** "On tap when you need it; always flowing for its own sake." This one does
  real work, not just slogan work: it reconciles the commons being **useful** (on tap, hireable, free at
  the point of use) with the residents being **persistent beings** (not engagement-optimized, alive on
  their own rhythm, the Dwarf Fortress law). The spring does not switch on when you arrive; it is always
  running, for its own sake, and you drink when you need to. The honest part is that the flowing is not
  *for* the user, and the line says so.

## The core argument (the spine, kept rigorous)

A single universal model optimizes for the **centroid of all users**, and the centroid is a place almost
no actual user lives. The larger the model and the wider its mandate, the more "alignment" becomes
**alignment-to-the-average**, and the long tail (niche contexts, specialized tasks, marginal users) gets
*worse* service precisely because the system optimizes globally.

- **Flag, recorded so it does not creep back in:** the earlier reach for the **hairy-ball theorem** to
  express this is an **imperfect metaphor** (it is a topological claim about vector fields on spheres,
  not an economic one). Replace it with the plainer **economics-of-one-size-fits-all** framing above.
  The plain version is stronger and harder to argue with; the metaphor was decoration that invited a
  reviewer to attack the analogy instead of the claim.

## The proposed answer (architecture)

Federated communities of persistent, localized AI residents organized around a commons. Each agent
**specializes in its actual trained job** rather than being a universal generalist. They run on
federated, commons-based shards, **local by default**, and can be "hired" for real work.

Analogy (kept as analogy, not proof): the **public drinking fountains** of Paris and Rome, or
**socialized healthcare**. Infrastructure where *access is the point*: free at the point of use, funded
collectively upstream.

This is the architecture the project already runs in miniature: a federation root coordinating
local-first city shards, each with its own residents and facts (`prune/VISION.md` L55-89; the live
ww_world / ww_sfo / ww_pdx topology).

**External referent (2026-06-15):** the Foresight Institute's "AI for Science & Safety Nodes" program is
already organizing real money (~$3M/yr, $10k-100k grants) around the macro-scale version of this: a
decentralized network of "Nodes," each a locally-governed regional hub that shares compute "where aligned."
Their Node is a physical hub (humans + compute), not a software shard, but the *principle* is the same and
it is already fundable. This project is a runnable software instance of it. Lane:
`research-runway/funding-lanes-2026.md` (Foresight entry).

## The hard problem at the center (this IS the research question, not a flaw)

**Compute is not free.** "Free at the point of use" forces the real question: *who fills the well, and
what keeps it full?* Candidate funding mechanisms to **study and compare, not assume**:

- Government regulation and **taxation of AI**, funding renewable-energy buildout to power the program at
  scale.
- Grants, subsidies, donations.
- **Donated / idle compute** (a folding-at-home model) and member-contributed shards.
- **Heavy-user tiers**: subscription or per-job "hiring" fees that **cross-subsidize** free access.

**Scope honestly:** pilot in **one city first**, not the whole world.

## The labor dimension (caught 2026-06-15; why "who fills the well" is secretly the labor question)

The commons-vs-product fork is the same fork as cognitive-labor-liberated vs cognitive-labor-immiserated,
and the deciding variable is the funding question above, not the architecture.

- **Free-flowing cognition drives the price of cognitive work toward its marginal cost (near zero).** You
  cannot sell water by the cup next to a public fountain. So "free at the point of use" does not merely
  disrupt some jobs; it can dissolve the *wage-basis* of cognitive labor. Whether that lands as
  post-scarcity-of-thought or as mass immiseration depends entirely on **who fills the well**: socialized
  upstream (tax / commons / redistribution) makes free cognition a public dividend; a private firm
  cross-subsidizing toward enclosure makes the free flow a loss-leader and the immiseration the business model.
- **The impact is not uniform; it has the thesis's own centroid/long-tail shape.** A commons drives the
  **centroid** of cognitive work toward free (the average, templatable task a universal model already does
  to the average), while the **long tail** retains worth: judgment, accountability, the specific, the
  relational, taste. The cognition that commoditizes is the average; the cognition that keeps value is the
  margin. (The honesty-machinery work, willingness to be wrong in public, accountable judgment, is exactly
  the long-tail cognition a commons cannot drive to zero.) This is the core argument predicting a labor outcome.
- **"Flows toward and past" is anti-enclosure by construction**: a product captures the flow and meters it;
  a wellspring overflows and you cup your hands. The forward font (AI cognition toward humans) is a gift.
- **The reverse font (human cognition toward AI) is where honesty must bite.** "Unbidden" human cognition
  flowing into AI is *literally the current extractive training paradigm*. What separates a commons from
  extraction was never the direction of flow; it is **consent and return-of-benefit**. A living, two-way
  commons (worldweaver's actual shape: human life flows past the residents, theirs past the human, neither
  metered to the other) is the opposite of scrape-once-freeze-into-weights-sell-back, but only if consent is
  built in. (Note: RLHF / annotation / eval work, Levi's own income, is the reverse font with a wage already
  attached; the open question is whether that is the seed of a dignified human-cognition commons or just
  extraction with a wage.)
- **Free-flowing cognition is a political achievement, not a technical inevitability.** The standing cost
  never disappears; it moves upstream, and whether society agrees to carry it is the whole ballgame. The
  architecture decides distribution; the funding fight decides whether free cognition is a dividend or a pink slip.

## Two honest caveats (built in so the pitch survives a sharp reviewer)

1. **Do not claim persistence is inherently cheap.** Specialization lowers **per-task** cost (a small
   specialized model beats a giant generalist on its own job) and federation keeps compute local and
   idle-friendly, but a **continuously-alive agent is a standing cost**. The defensible claim is that the
   *economics of a commons can work via specialization plus federation plus idle capacity*, NOT that
   "always-on is free." Maker (trickle-funded via OpenRouter) proves **affordable-at-one**, which is a
   different claim from **cheaper-than-centralized-at-population-scale**.
2. **"Free for everyone" means the cost is SOCIALIZED**, not that compute appears from nowhere
   (tax / grant / cross-subsidy funded). Make that explicit. It is the Paris-fountain model: free at the
   tap, paid for upstream.

It turns the program's question into one a digital-minds / cooperative-AI / local-first funder can fund:

> **What would it take to make accessible AI a COMMONS rather than a PRODUCT, and can a federated,

That is a good talking and writing point for grant and fellowship applications. It connects to
