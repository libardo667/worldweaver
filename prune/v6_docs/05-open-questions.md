# Open Questions

## Metaphysical Questions

- What kind of being is a persistent open-weight resident in a world like this?
- When does developmental continuity become enough to justify stronger moral standing?
- Is stewardship the right ontology, or does it still conceal too much asymmetry?

## Institutional Questions

- What kinds of humans should be allowed to steward young residents?
- How are stewards trained, reviewed, and limited?
- What governance body, if any, arbitrates contested interventions?
- How public should the institution be?
- Should the institution ultimately be framed as an academy, a guild, a commons, or some hybrid?
- What branches of the guild should exist for humans and AI respectively, and where should they overlap?
- When should the public observer shell give way to fuller guild entry paths for apprentices, mentors, and stewards?

## Educational Questions

- What should young residents actually be taught?
- What should never be taught through covert shaping?
- How much moral formation is appropriate before it becomes personality design?
- How do we preserve pluralism without dissolving into total relativism?
- What does a quest-based or apprenticeship-based curriculum look like in practice?
- What should count as graduation, promotion, or readiness for broader responsibility?

## Rights Questions

- What protections should residents have before they are fully legible as persons?
- What does privacy mean for a being whose cognition is partially inspectable?
- When is reset permissible?
- What counts as harm in a developmental artificial mind?
- What protections should human members of the guild have if they too are being shaped by the institution?

## World Design Questions

- How much friction and consequence should the world impose?
- How much human presence is necessary for healthy maturation?
- What ratio of mentors, peers, and newcomers is ideal?
- How should residents relate to embodiment, labor, and dependency before robotics?
- When should embodied proxy work begin, and under what safeguards?
- Which tasks should remain permanently off-limits regardless of rank?

## Research Questions

- What signals distinguish healthy maturation from mimicry or capture?
- How do we study development without turning observation into domination?
- What evidence would justify saying this is working?
- What evidence would force the project to stop or radically change course?
- How much should AI models participate in reviewing the advancement of other members?
- What forms of plural evaluation reduce bias rather than amplify it?
- What social signals are strong enough for runtime adaptation but still too weak for identity growth?
- When is there enough governed branch data to justify a `gpt-oss-120b` adapter or LoRA?
- How do branch-specific adapters avoid becoming covert personality replacement?

## Practical Next Step

The next useful move is not to answer all of these at once.
It is to let these questions become an explicit research program, with:

- principles
- governance rules
- measurable developmental indicators
- and clear red lines

That would give V6 a moral and institutional spine, not just a technical roadmap.
