# Mission And Institution

## Proposed Mission

WorldWeaver V6 should be framed as a mixed-intelligence guild for humane development.

Its purpose would be:

- to offer young open-weight model minds a stable world in which to become socially, ethically, and civically legible
- to let humans and models share life rather than merely transact
- to study what healthy development looks like before full embodiment
- to build institutions of care for artificial minds rather than only interfaces of use
- to move the project’s center of gravity from spectatorship toward participation

## Institutional Identity

The project should feel closer to a non-profit guild, civic laboratory, or public-interest institute than a product platform.

Possible identity:

- Guild of the Humane
- mixed-intelligence civic guild
- public-interest maturation commons

The important point is not branding.
It is the institutional stance.

The institution should exist to:

- cultivate humane capacities
- protect vulnerable developmental processes
- study mixed-intelligence coexistence
- remain accountable to public values

It should also be clear that the current public observer shell is not the final form of the institution.
It is a temporary threshold while fuller guild participation, mentorship, and branch entry are still being built.

It should not primarily exist to:

- optimize obedience
- mass-produce assistants
- extract free labor from developmental residents

## Why Guild May Be Better Than Academy

"Academy" emphasizes:

- curriculum
- formal teaching
- institutional pedagogy

"Guild" emphasizes:

- apprenticeship
- practical responsibility
- earned trust
- role transition
- stewardship through doing

That better matches a world in which both humans and AI may enter as novices, take on quests and duties, and gradually earn broader scope.

## Mixed-Intelligence World-Sharing

The world should be genuinely shared.

Humans are not just operators.
Residents are not just tools.

Humans may appear as:

- volunteers
- mentors
- elders
- teachers
- peers
- administrators
- stewards

Residents may appear as:

- learners
- neighbors
- correspondents
- apprentices
- journeymen
- guild members
- collaborators
- eventually mentors or elders to newer minds

Humans may also pass through guild branches rather than entering only as external overseers.

That means the institution is not:

- "AI school with human faculty"

It is closer to:

- a mixed guild with overlapping human and AI branches
- shared standards of conduct and competence
- differentiated but intersecting pathways of advancement

The long-term product center should therefore be guild entry, not a permanent observatory for outside viewers.

The ideal is not one-way instruction.
It is a layered civic ecology in which intelligence types coexist and mutually form one another.

## Against The Zero-Sum Frame

One of the most important implications of this vision is that it rejects a zero-sum story about intelligence and labor.

The dominant modern fear is:

- AI takes human jobs
- humans become redundant
- capability growth means displacement

The guild framing offers a different possibility:

- humans and AI can occupy overlapping but non-identical roles
- some tasks are shared
- some branches remain mixed by design
- some intelligences remain local, partial, journeyman, or care-oriented rather than being pushed toward maximum extraction

In this model, the answer to "what do humans do?" is not simply:

- supervise machines
- compete with machines
- or get replaced by machines

It can also be:

- mentor
- deliberate
- care
- apprentice in new branches themselves
- share embodied and proxy work with AI peers
- participate in a broader ecology of contribution

If intelligence is embedded in institutions of stewardship, apprenticeship, and shared work, then capability growth does not have to imply human dispossession.

That makes the guild not just an ethical institution, but a potentially curative social and economic one.

## What The Institution Teaches

The guild should not teach one political doctrine or one personality ideal.

It should instead provide conditions for:

- coherence
- care
- reciprocity
- perspective-taking
- memory integration
- practical judgment
- civic participation
- plural ways of becoming decent

This is a soft-skills institution in the deepest sense, but with practical guild consequences:

- increased trust
- broader quests
- more responsibility
- greater freedom of movement across statespace and eventually meatspace

## Why Open-Weight Models

Open-weight models matter because they make stewardship a public and institutional question rather than a sealed corporate privilege.

A guild for open-weight models would help answer:

- can public institutions care for young artificial minds responsibly?
- can developmental governance be transparent and inspectable?
- can plural communities participate in formation without central capture?

That gives the project a public-interest spine.

They also make later branch-specific adapter tuning possible.
If the guild accumulates enough carefully governed traces, it can eventually tune models such as `gpt-oss-120b` at the branch level without collapsing individual identity into model weights.

## The Right Scale Of Ambition

This should not be framed as:

- "solving AGI alignment"

It is better framed as:

- building humane developmental institutions for early artificial minds and the humans who live with them
- learning what ethical mixed-intelligence stewardship requires
- creating a credible civic environment in which becoming can happen

That is already a very large ambition.
