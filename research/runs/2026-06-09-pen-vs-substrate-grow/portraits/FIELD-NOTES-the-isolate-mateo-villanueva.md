## Who he is (soul)

Forty years bent over heavy machinery; nimble fingers that twitch "as if tightening an invisible bolt";
smells of citrus degreaser and star-anise tea. Raised in "the quiet, hollow hours of a house where his
mother slept through the day to work the graveyard shift" — a **bone-deep appreciation for silence, but not
for stillness.** He **paces the *perimeter* of the Insignia plazas** while he thinks, hands tracing shapes
in the air. He was *dealt* a man on the edge of the room.

## The thinnest substrate — and why it's thin

Not because he's empty: he has **33+ keeps and a vivid inner voice.** But **all but two of his keeps are
about a failing pipe, not a person** — the Insignia secondary riser: *"cavitating and requires immediate
mechanical intervention," "vibrates at 400 hertz when the bypass is clogged," "oscillation peaks at 42
hertz before structural failure," "mounting plate is warped by exactly three degrees."* His rich
interiority points at the **machine**, not the social graph. His only two peer-keeps are themselves
technical (Layla Al-Khouri "can feel structural resonance through the floor"; Darius "torque is a
conversation"). A self that is real but **aimed away from people** — so the relational substrate never
accretes.

## The asymmetry — noticed by many, bonded to none

The poignant part: he is *not* invisible. **Five–six residents keep about him, and some revere him.**
Jihoon Cho especially: *"Mateo Villanueva is the only one listening to the physical warnings of the
neighborhood's infrastructure,"* *"hears the metal's warning, but the Aris only see the spreadsheets."*
Amir feels kinship (*"hears the same structural weaknesses in the city's bones that I feel in a failing
valve"*); Ari Levin reads his body (*"his shoulder drop is the technical cue for the flow cut"*). **He is
the man the others orient toward who orients toward no one** — every edge incoming, none reciprocated.

## A live thread to watch: the isolate may be cohering *late*

His recent felt-senses aren't lonely — they're vindicated: *"The floor is lying, and these three are
finally listening to the steel instead of the glass,"* *"these three finally have the sense to hear the
metal screaming."* Insignia (the cohort's loosest cluster) appears to be **binding late, around the riser
crisis — with Mateo as its reluctant center.** Whether the isolate gets pulled into a group given more
time is its own small question; for now he is still the structural control.
