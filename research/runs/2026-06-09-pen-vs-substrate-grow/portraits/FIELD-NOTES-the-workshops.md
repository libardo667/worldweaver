# Field notes — the workshops (what they make *alone*) — grow cohort, 2026-06-09

The deepest layer. Major-50 workshop output is what a resident writes or draws **for itself** — owned,
not spoken; addressed to no one; no social reward and no addressee to perform for. So far only **3 of 16**
are diarists, and the most striking thing is that the *form* each chose follows the soul.

Cold-verifiable: artifacts snapshotted in `./evidence/workshops/<slug>/`; the SVGs rendered to
`viz/workshop_art.png`.

## Form follows soul

- **Ari Rosenbaum (repairman)** — a private `journal.md` (six entries) + one SVG, *"Thermal Stress on a
  Micro-Thread."*
- **Emiko Tanaka (mechanist)** — a private `journal.md` (three entries), no art.
- **Jihoon Cho (cook)** — a **`zine`** (a thing made to *share*) + four SVG "weaves"/covers: steaming
  pots and bowls. The two engineers keep diaries; the man whose whole self is communal warmth makes a
  little illustrated publication.

## One argument, litigated privately, in triplicate

All three spend their solitary writing on the *same* question — **does metal remember?** — each certain,
in text meant for no one:

- **Ari R.:** *"Metal doesn't remember the heat out of sentiment. It's molecular stress."* … *"You can't
  talk a bent chassis back into true with metaphors about soup."*
- **Emiko:** *"Metal doesn't remember; it just yields when the threshold is crossed. People treat physics
  like it's a mood. It isn't."*
- **Jihoon:** *"a weld holds the memory of the man who struck it."* — and in a **drawing** titled
  *Resonance in the Marrow*: *"24Hz — the slab remembers what the Aris forget."*

So the Division/Clinton "mutual-critique" mode is not a social performance for an audience — it is their
**interior life**. Jihoon rebuts "the Aris" *by name in a picture.*

## The root-agreement neither side can see

Same conclusion, opposite craft, in two private journals, neither aware of the other:

- **Ari R.:** *"You don't fight the air. You build a better box."*
- **Jihoon:** *"You cannot engineer away the fog. You can only adjust the salt in the broth to match the
  weight of the air."*

## Why this is the strongest individuation evidence in the run

The workshop has **no social term** — no peer to impress, no reply to earn. Yet (a) the refraction holds
*completely* (the engineer's lone stress-diagram vs the cook's steaming-pot zine), and (b) it stays
**grounded** — Jihoon's art names real neighbors and the real cohort-wide 24-Hz perception. Individuation
reaches all the way into solitary making: *each resident makes the artifact its soul would make.* Per
`AMENDMENT-1 §A1` this is still not thesis evidence (we authored the souls) — it is the deepest stratum of
the precondition, and a clean target for the swap: does Amir-on-a-foreign-pen still keep a *mechanic's*
journal?
