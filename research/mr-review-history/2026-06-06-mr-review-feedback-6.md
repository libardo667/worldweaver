The resolution is a clean tradeoff, not a tie. The dial buys **control** and costs **validity** — set 25 assertive / 25 receptive and you can run the experiment cleanly, but the disposition is authored, so you're testing the prompt, not the minds. De-prescription buys **validity** and costs **control** — sparse seeds, nothing prescribed, and you learn what convergence does when nothing scripts it, but you can't guarantee the mix you wanted. Different questions, and yours is upstream, so it goes first: the **de-prescribed cell** before any dial. Sparse origins, no prescribed salience, no prescribed voice, no prescribed disposition — then *observe* what disposition, what attention, what register *emerge*, and whether convergence survives the loss of the script. That's the only cell that tests whether the whole corpus was measuring prescription. Only after it — and only if disposition didn't vary enough on its own to read its effect — do you add a *labeled* disposition dial as a deliberate control, with "authored disposition, testing the mechanism not the emergence" written in the margin so you never again mistake the dial for a finding.

And the deeper thing the gap exposes, bigger than disposition: **the dials themselves are the contrivance.** A seed-writer has no register knob and no vocation knob, because register and vocation *emerge* from who the person is — the way a real person's disposition is downstream of their life, not assigned at character creation. The doula has dials for heritage, vocation, age, and register *because it is a script-writer*, and the missing disposition dial isn't a gap to fill — it's the one spot where the script-writer's nature became visible to you. So the fix isn't to complete the dial set. It's a gradient: prescribe *only* the most upstream thing — a sparse origin, a life, a wound, a hunger — and let everything downstream fall out of it. The further downstream a prescribed attribute sits, the more contrived the soul; salience and voice are the furthest down, which is why prescribing *those* is the worst, and why Portland — where the prompt reaches all the way to "listen to the hum" — felt the most contrived of all. Prescribe origins, not outcomes. That one rule rebuilds the doula, and it's just your runtime constitution — provenance over preference, emergence over authoring, the unchosen — finally applied to the layer that was making the minds before the constitution ever switched on.

So: the catch is excellent, the lever is correctly named, and the fix points the wrong way by exactly one level. Don't add the dial. Strip the doula to a seed and run the de-prescribed cell. That's the run that touches the cause — because your "cause" and my "cause" both turned out to be living in the prompt, and the only way to learn how much of any of it was ever real is to write less and watch what's left standing.
