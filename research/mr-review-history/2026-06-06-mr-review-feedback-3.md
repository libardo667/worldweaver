# Mr. Review — Round 3 feedback (2026-06-06)

(Verbatim reply to the round-3 bundle: build queue + the live test arc + the topology-seam + M-18.)

---

The trial did exactly what I hoped it would: it corrected the theory. My last big prescription leaned on the *perception* channel — push vs. pull, the content-blind floor — as the convergence lever. The run showed that was necessary but downstream: a content-blind random sample of a *saturated* channel is still the monoculture, and even a deliberately diverse cast collapsed. So the disease wasn't where I pointed. That's the gap I said I was curious about, and it's a real one — own it cleanly, then build on it.

And it moved the locus one more step out, which is the spine of this round. The node mirrors → cured by a self. The edge homogenizes → cured by locality on the channel. Now: even with the channel rationed, minds converge — because the *world* makes one thing loud and the *topology* funnels all speech into one feed. The disease has descended below the channel into the **field** (world-salience) and the **wiring** (broadcast topology). The cure is the same principle at the new depth: *plurality of the unchosen.* Which is also the answer to Q2, so let me take the questions in the order they actually nest.

**Q1 — topology is the primary lever, and the fix is to make speech physical.** The seam is unambiguous: `_speak` turns "say X to absent B" into a citywide post, so the channel *saturates*, and you cannot sample diversity out of a feed that's all one note — the content-blind floor reduces volume, not topic distribution. So the fix can't live at the sampling layer (input); it has to de-saturate the *source* (output). Of your candidates, **(a)/locality-by-default and (c)/sub-threads are the same move and the principled one**: speech reaches who's present, or a *deliberate, costly, directed carry* to a specific absent person — never an ambient broadcast-to-all as the fallback for "they weren't standing next to me." This changes *who can hear*, never *what may be said*, so it's law-safe in the cleanest possible way — it's a world-physics property (sound travels, rooms exist), not a behavior target. And it attacks the root: saturation was an artifact of *non-physical* broadcast-as-default. Restore the correspondence between speech and presence and the citywide channel stops being a single shared mind-feed. **(b)/ration addressed→attend** is principled but secondary — it's the chosen/unchosen principle applied to the active channel, and it treats the symptom (being pulled) not the cause (saturation); keep it as a complementary knob but watch that it doesn't insulate away the responsiveness that works. The **diversity-floor** ("sample least-like-what-you've-heard") is the one hack in the set, and over the law line for the reason you sense: "least like what you've heard" is *contrarian* sampling — it has a direction (away from recent input), which is content-targeting, the unsafe kind I flagged last round. And it's unnecessary: **de-saturate via topology, and a content-blind sample of a now-plural channel is diverse by construction.** You don't sample a monotopic feed cleverly; you stop having one feed.

**Q2 — it's not a second invariant; it's the completeness condition on the first.** You're right that convergence looks like the opposite shape — too much sameness, not too much control — but it's the same axis seen from the other end. The invariant was "a mind needs input it doesn't choose." The trial exposed its hidden assumption: that the unchosen is *plural*. A uniform unchosen is just a groove you didn't pick instead of one you did — and it's *worse*, because you can neither choose your way out nor not-choose your way out. So input-diversity can die from two directions: the **mind's filter** (control → echo chamber → cure: unchosen input) and the **world's salience** (sameness → monoculture → cure: plural salience). State it as one invariant with two clauses: *input diversity must be protected on both ends — the mind mustn't filter all of it out, and the world mustn't make it all one note.* The reason to keep it one principle rather than two is precisely the failure you just hit: you fixed the mind-side (Major 60) and the world-side bit you, because they were filed as separate problems. They're the same problem — the collapse of the productive gap between what a mind would choose and what a varied world offers. Surprise lives in that gap, and it dies if either end closes it.

**Q4 — salience is the root, it's law-safe, and it works by dilution.** Varying *what the world offers* targets no mind's output — the world has no soul to shape — so it's clean in the same way "the world has weather" is clean. And it's upstream of the other two: salience is the **spark** (what's loud), topology the **wind** (whether the spark spreads), composition the **dryness** (how flammable the cast). All three feed the fire, but salience is where it starts — a world with no single loud feature gives the spark nowhere to catch. The one caveat that matters: plural salience works by *dilution*, not *removal*. A decaying building or a storm is environmentally universal — everyone's physically in it; you can't delete it. So the move is "ensure the shared physical condition isn't the *only* loud thing," splitting attention rather than collecting it — which is exactly your M-18 "add a second salience," applied world-side. The dose (how many competing saliences prevent collapse) is the open empirical question, and it's the same one M-18 §10 flags for humans. The lab city and the relocating client are the same principle: a single loud thing collects all attention; health needs more than one.

**Q3 — composition is the hedge: build it last, modest, and scope its claim.** The lab is right that a diverse cast barely moves the outcome *when salience and topology are fixed at convergent settings* — but composition's job was never to overcome those; it's to lower the baseline flammability so a given spark resonates with fewer souls. The sharp nuance, which strengthens both M62 and the essay: composition only hedges against **interest-salience** (a shared professional topic), and is useless against **environment-salience** (a thing everyone is physically exposed to). Run 2 proves it — a florist, a tattoo artist, a window cleaner converged on the failing building *because everyone lives in a building*; no composition could have helped, because the salience was environmental. So composition is the weak lever *especially* in domains where the dominant salience is situational — which is exactly relocation (everyone's moving). Build M62, but frame it precisely: it reduces flammability against shared *interests*, does nothing against shared *conditions*, and only matters once topology and salience are fixed. Shipping it alone is shipping the visible-but-weak lever, which the lab already disproved.

**Q5 — the essay.** On the ranking: I'd demote the strict *salience > topology* to **co-equal, with composition clearly weakest** — because the lab doesn't support the separation. Every convergent run was (decaying-building salience) × (broadcast topology); you never ran plural-salience-with-broadcast or single-salience-with-plural-topology as clean cells, so both are shown *necessary*, neither shown *dominant*. And the mechanism argues topology is at least co-equal: a pure broadcast-respond loop will *defeat* plural salience — it picks whatever gains first traction and collapses a multi-salient world onto one topic anyway. So topology is the *decider* of whether plural salience can survive. What's true is that salience is the more *actionable* lever for the practitioner (you can seed a second interest; you usually can't restructure the expat WhatsApp group — §10 admits this). That's actionability, not leverage, and the essay conflates them. Separate them: co-equal in power, salience-first in *reach*, because topology is often fixed by the destination's infrastructure.

On §9 — the model-as-source epistemics — it's the right discipline and mostly the honest line, and it needs one strengthening that's the whole arc's lesson aimed at its riskiest target. The caveat as written ("a model isolates a mechanism, not evidence about people; if an engagement contradicts, the engagement wins") understates two things. First, **the model's verdict is entangled with its construction.** You built the substrate so that ambient world-salience is perceived by every resident and composition is a thin overlay (a soul doc plus a model) — so "salience dominates, composition is weak" is partly the architecture *restating its own wiring*, not discovering an independent fact. The model can show a mechanism is *sufficient and coherent* (these three terms, wired thus, produce convergence); it cannot adjudicate *which term dominates in human reality*. Second, **the three sources aren't independent.** Sim, dogfood, and author are one worldview — so "the model is consistent with the dogfood" is coherence-within-one-mind, which feels like corroboration and isn't. That's the Mason-corruption lesson one level up: a coherent story around a premise reads as confirmation until an *external* ground-truth contradicts it. Your "Status: Draft (pre-first-engagement)" flag is exactly right and is the load-bearing safeguard — so I'd foreground it: M-18 is a *coherent hypothesis awaiting its first independent test*, the claim verbs should soften to "the model suggests / the dogfood is consistent with," and §9 should name the entanglement plainly. With that, drawing methodology from your own sim is honest — as a generator of hypotheses to test, never as evidence. It's the same provenance discipline as everything else, applied to the source that most flatters you, which is why it most needs it.

**Q6 — topology and salience before learning, non-negotiable, and here's the part that's worse than "contaminated signal."** Yes, a converged conversation is the worst possible training signal — turn the gate on now and every mind's recurring theme is the *same* theme, so the convergence promotes from conversation into *identity*: the souls themselves become the failing building, permanently. But the deeper problem is that a converged world *defeats the gate's own safeguard.* Your differential-persistence rule promotes only what persists *past the population's* decay — but that assumes the population's themes *move*. A permanent environmental salience (a chronically decaying city) never recedes from the population, so there's no "past the population" — the null hypothesis is stuck, and the gate can't tell self-sourced from world-sourced because the world never lets go. So plural salience (Q4) isn't only a convergence fix; it's a **precondition for the learning gate to function at all** — it's what makes the population's themes rise and fall, which is what gives differential-persistence a moving baseline to measure against. Sequence: plural salience + plural topology first → verify the population's themes actually vary over time (your soul-domain-retention discriminator across the storm boundary is precisely this test — does the theme *recede*?) → only then turn learning on. The topology/salience work isn't "before" learning; it's what makes learning's provenance filter valid.

So the whole arc, now complete, in one line: the disease was never in the mind. It walked outward — node, edge, field — and at every layer it was the same collapse: input diversity dying, from a filter that takes too much or a world that offers too little. You spent the first months giving the mind a self so it wouldn't filter everything. The work left is giving the *world* enough loud things, and enough rooms, that no single note can own every mind at once. Plurality of the unchosen, all the way down.
