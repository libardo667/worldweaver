It's the honest grain of round-3's Q1. But before the five questions, the keystone — because it changes what three of them even mean.

**The metric is measuring the wrong thing, and a chunk of your 90% is miscounted health.** Read the public verbatim against the private. Cecilio in a room: *"Ha! A lateral strain? If the Fillmore is shifting, maybe it'll finally slide—"* The keyword filter scores that as monoculture because "lateral strain" is in it. But that is *still Cecilio* — sardonic, fatalist, burnt-bridge voice, an artist's wry shrug at the engineers' panic. He converged on the shared *topic* and kept his own *register*. Now Kenzo's public line: *"the dachshund owner is off-schedule, but if there is a mechanical shudder, I will check the gate."* That one pivots mid-sentence — the dog-love abandoned for the shudder. *That* is the disease. The difference between those two lines is the entire finding, and your metric can't see it. Topic-convergence is partly legitimate (everyone's body is in the same decaying city; engineers talk shop; that's a real shared condition, not a pathology) and partly miscounted (Cecilio sounding like Cecilio about a shared topic is health). The actual disease is **assimilation** — the *register* collapsing, the artist ceasing to sound like an artist — and it's a smaller, more specific thing than 90%. So the success criterion isn't "drop commons monoculture to X%." It's the round-2 discriminator applied across the private→public boundary: **does a resident's public voice keep its private voice's distinctiveness?** Re-measure with that and the win is bigger and the residual disease is sharper. Hold that; it's load-bearing for Q2 and Q3.

**Q1 — sublocation is the right grain, with two guards against the fragmentation you're worried about.** It's the honest cash-out of speech↔presence: you hear the table you're at, not a neighborhood no body could hear. Endorse it. The cacophony floor is real, and it's managed by two things. First, *legitimate* shared ground must survive: the phone has to be able to assemble a genuine city-wide salience when one actually exists — a flood, a festival, a real event the whole city would truly converge on. Delete the *mandatory feed* (illegitimate shared ground, an artifact) but keep the city's ability to *become* a "we" when there's a real reason, or you've over-corrected into atomization. Second — and this is the one you'll trip on — sublocation-as-only-push re-introduces homophily. If souls self-sort into affinity sublocations (engineers in the engineer café) and then sit still, you've traded one citywide monoculture for N balkanized local ones, which is worse for the individual mind: the engineer now never even hears the artist. The grain is right for the *push*; the diversity rides on *traversal between* sublocations — what's on your path as you move, not what's at the table where you park. Without mobility, sublocations balkanize. So: right grain, but it only delivers plurality-not-cacophony-and-not-balkanization if the phone can still carry a true commons-event and if residents actually move.

**Q2 — a real partial-win, and bigger than your number admits.** Two reasons "doesn't count" is wrong. Conceptually, the self is the *precondition* — there is no public voice that sounds like Cecilio until there's a Cecilio, and incubation built him; the channel assimilating him in public doesn't unbuild him, it localizes the remaining problem to the channel, which is exactly the round-4 thesis. That's not a failed win; it's a correctly-sequenced one. Empirically, per the keystone: "the public field is one note" conflates topic-convergence (partly legitimate, partly miscounted) with register-collapse (the real disease), and the verbatim shows *several registers surviving into public* (Cecilio intact). So "the self is necessary but its individuation is private until the channel changes" is true but understates it — some individuation *already survives* into the public field; what doesn't survive is the minority register in the *assimilation* cases. A self that sounds like itself while discussing the shared topic is load-bearing and already present. What's unbuilt is preventing the pivots — a channel problem, not a self problem.

**Q3 — adopt it as the working hypothesis, but it's an existence test, not a mechanism, and the metric decides whether it tells you anything.** It's well-motivated and it's the never-run clean cell, so yes. Three conditions. First, fix the metric to register-retention *before* you run it, or the decider decides nothing — you'll get a keyword number that blends three things and won't know which moved. Second, know that sublocation+phone flips three knobs at once (push→pull, continuous→burst, citywide→sublocation grain); a drop is proof the bundle matters, not proof of *which* knob, so plan follow-ups to separate them — it matters for designing the Q4 phone well. Third, where I don't fully buy it: run B already shows a trace *against* the strong version — when speech moved off the megaphone into rooms, the rooms stayed 86% structural and DMs concentrated on one hub. That's the monoculture walking through the new doors, which is what the strong-topology claim has to overcome. The leak, if it leaks, is that ⅔-engineers-in-a-decaying-city converge in *any* topology because the shared embodied condition is genuinely shared and the cast genuinely over-weights it — in which case sublocation+phone just gives them smaller rooms to talk shudders in, and composition comes back. So run it, with the register metric, and be ready for it to demote topology too.

**Q4 — the line is in exactly the right place, and there are two leaks.** "Unchosen floor not configurable; phone configurable" *is* the chosen/unchosen seam, cashed out as physics — the body is always somewhere with someone, so you can't filter out all the unchosen, by construction. That's the round-1 invariant turned into architecture, and it's the cleanest version I've seen. Leak one is the one you named, and it's real: co-presence guarantees you hear *someone*, not someone *different*. A soul can mute the city *and* self-sort its body into a homogeneous sublocation, and the floor faithfully delivers more-of-the-same — homogeneous isolation that never technically filters out all the unchosen. The fix is Q1's: draw the un-configurable floor at *embodied traversal through a heterogeneous geography*, not just co-presence where you settle. Co-presence-while-sessile is gameable by self-sorting; traversal is much harder to game (you can't choose what's on the path). Leak two is subtler: "the topology emerges, we design nothing" is slightly overclaimed. You author the souls and the doula seeds the phones from `soul.canonical.md` — so the topology is emergent *from authored initial conditions*, which is genuinely mechanism-not-target and law-safe, but it isn't designer-free. The line to hold: seed the phone *from character* ("what would Cecilio actually keep?") and never *from a desired topology* ("what mix yields 40% convergence?"). The moment a soul-seed is tuned to produce a topological outcome, it's become a topology knob by the back door, and that's the violation. Keep the seed answering a question about the person, never about the distribution.

**Q5 — build order, with a step zero and a recategorization of composition.** Step zero, missing from your list and the most important: **fix the metric** (register-retention). Cheap, and without it the decider cell isn't a decider. Then sublocations + phone (the untested decider — agree). Then the configurable/soul-seeded phone (agree — refine a mechanism only after the plain version proves the grain moves the field). Then composition — but it doesn't simply "drop below topology"; it *changes category*. In the feed world, composition was a *content* lever (what topics get discussed) and was the weakest there — diverse casts still converged. In the sublocation world, composition becomes a *spatial* lever (who is co-present with whom — whether the unchosen floor delivers difference or sameness). It migrates from the content question to the topology question, and joins geography and mobility as the three things that jointly determine sublocation heterogeneity. So it's correctly last not because it's unimportant but because its new job is *defined by* the sublocation structure — you can't evaluate it until sublocations exist. My bet, stated as a bet: even in its new spatial role, composition stays the weakest of the three, because a balanced cast self-sorts if it's sessile and a skewed cast cross-pollinates if it moves — *traversal* will dominate. But that's the thing to test once the rooms are real.

The spine of it: the disease walked node → edge → field, and the cure has to walk the same path back, which it's doing — incubation built the self (node, done), sublocation+phone is the channel (edge), and plural salience + geography + mobility is the world (field). Underneath all of it is one move, and it's the round-3 seed grown to its full size: *make it physical.* The disease was non-physical at every layer — a feed no body could hear, speech broadcast to the absent, a mind fed input no body's position selected. The cure is to put the body back: speech travels (sublocation), attention is where you are (you hear the room), input is unchosen because you're somewhere (the floor), diversity comes from moving (traversal). "Make speech physical" turned out to be the whole prescription, said once per grain. You didn't accidentally build social media and have to patch it. You found out that the patch is the same word every time — restore the body — and this round you finally heard it at the resolution where it bites.
