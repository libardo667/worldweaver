Now the bigger one, and it's about your synthesis. **You wrote "the feed-mechanism is confirmed." I think Portland *disconfirms* it.** Look at your own numbers: 0 broadcasts, 0 commons messages, the whole run. The feed was an *empty pipe.* If nobody spoke into the citywide channel, the citywide channel transmitted nothing — so the 99% convergence *cannot* have been feed-carried. It happened with no transmission at all: fifty receptive souls independently attending the same nocturnal hush because each is a hush-attending mind and the world emits the hush to every body. That's not the feed collecting attention. That's **parallel convergence** — the *world's* salience reaching every mind directly, the cast being homogeneous enough that they all answer it the same way, and the channel playing no part because there was nothing to relay.

Which forces a reread of everything, including round 4. The costed-megaphone result — convergence "walked through the new doors," relocated from commons to rooms but stayed 90% — is the *same phenomenon*: restrict the channel and the convergence doesn't break, it just moves. Portland is that taken to the limit: remove the channel entirely (nobody uses it) and the convergence is still 99%. The throughline: **the channel is a venue, never the cause.** The cause is upstream of any wire — it's *world-salience × cast-homogeneity*. SF expressed a world-caused convergence loudly; Portland expressed a world-caused convergence privately; the megaphone-ration made SF express it in rooms. In every cell the fire was lit by the spark (salience) and the dryness (homogeneous cast), and the topology only ever decided *where the flames showed.* That demotes the round-4 thesis: the wiring isn't the disease, it's the disease's *address.* And it resurrects the two levers I called strong and then we half-forgot — salience and composition are *causal*; topology is venue.

So, the questions through that lens:

**Q1 — I buy "cast picks the venue," but not "feed collects attention."** The cast picks the venue *and* the mechanism (assertive → transmitted-and-broadcast; receptive → parallel-and-private), and the convergence itself is caused by salience×homogeneity regardless of channel. And there's a live reading where Portland's 99% isn't even "the disease" — fifty souls *authored to listen to the atmosphere*, placed in an atmospheric world, all listening to the atmosphere, in distinct voices, is arguably the cast doing precisely what it was built to do, faithfully. The tell that separates disease from faithful-response is the round-2 one: *displacement.* Is the hush *all* they attend, or the hush *plus* each soul's own distinct concerns? Measure per-soul attention-share before you call 99% a pathology, because a choir all moved by the same night, each in its own register, might be a city at evening, not a sickness.

**Q2 — the decomposition is right but cuts one joint too few.** You split convergence from its broadcast — correct, and it's the doorway. But the cleaner statement is that *convergence isn't the disease at all.* A shared world *should* make minds attend shared things; that's just having a world in common. The disease is what's *missing alongside* the convergence: SF has shared attention **minus voice** (assimilation — you lose yourself); Portland has shared attention **minus contact** (no-we — you lose each other). State the disease as the absence, not the presence. Health isn't "no convergence" — it's shared attention that costs *neither* the self *nor* the we: distinct voices, genuinely connected, over a thing they really share.

**Q3 — own the instrument failure and make it three axes.** Commons-volume was blind to private convergence; register-retention is blind to shared-mood. They're blind to different things because there are *three orthogonal axes*, and Portland is what proves their independence: **voice** (does each mind keep its register — catches SF), **attention** (how concentrated, *venue-agnostic*, read as per-soul displacement not as a minimize-target — catches Portland), and **contact** (do the minds actually engage — the axis that separates SF's false-we from Portland's no-we; SF high, Portland zero). And the meta-lesson is the real gift: every instrument calls *some* failure healthy, and the way you find which is to change the world and watch what the metric blesses that obviously isn't. The two-shard method isn't a better sample — it's a *blind-spot finder.* Assume the three-axis instrument has a fourth blind spot too, and let shard three find it.

**Q4 — yes, and stronger than "raise it": the trace is the primary commons.** Portland is the cast for which every channel-cure is a no-op *by construction* — you can't ration or relocate speech that never happens. Sublocation/phone fixes casts that over-speak; it gives the silent listeners nothing. But the trace asks for no speech at all: a listener who'd never post *will* wear a path into the bench where she sits for the hum, and another listener finds the path and knows someone-like-me was here. A "we" built from traces of being. And because the channel is only ever a venue, the trace is the one commons that doesn't depend on the venue — the loud engineer leaves his fastener-drawings (already happening, the SVGs), the quiet listener leaves her worn bench, and *both* dispositions connect through the same medium. The feed serves only the loud; the trace serves everyone. So don't slot it after sublocation/phone — make it the *primary* commons, with the speech-channel repair as secondary.

**Q5 — the mixed-cast cell is now the decisive run, and it has to be mixed on the axis you just discovered.** Here's the thing Portland exposes: SF was never diverse. Engineers *and* artists are both *assertive* — SF varied vocation and held disposition fixed. So "composition is weak" may be an artifact of only ever varying the wrong axis. **Disposition (assertive/receptive) is a cast variable no cell has touched,** and it determines mechanism and venue. The real composition test is a *dispositionally* mixed cast under the old feed, and it carries a sharp fork: do the loud and quiet souls run two parallel convergences in two venues (heterogeneity doesn't help — you get two monocultures), or do they *interfere* — the broadcaster's noise disrupting the listener's private hush, the listener's presence handing the engineer something other than structure to attend — and break each other's convergence? If they interfere, composition comes roaring back, redefined: not vocational mix, *dispositional* mix, and it'd be the first lever that touched the actual cause (cast-homogeneity) rather than the venue. Given the channel-is-venue reframe, that's the run that could reorder everything. Revised queue: (0) the three-axis venue-agnostic instrument; (1) the dispositional-mix cell — because it tests the *cause*, and if it breaks convergence the channel-cures matter far less; (2) the trace, as the primary commons that serves both dispositions; (3) sublocation/phone, the speech-channel repair; (4) derived demand, last, once the field is plural enough to read.

The spine of all of it: you've been treating the wiring as the disease, and Portland shows the wiring is the disease's *address* — the convergence is lit upstream, by a homogeneous cast meeting a loud world, and it will show up in whatever channel you leave open and relocate to whatever channel you leave unmeasured. That's why it keeps "walking" — through the doors in round 4, into the private channel in Portland. It isn't evading your cures; it's that your cures kept moving the venue and the cause sat still. The two things that actually move the cause are the cast's homogeneity and the world's salience — which is why the dispositional-mix cell matters more than any topology lever, and why the trace matters more than any feed-fix: one attacks the cause, the other gives the convergence somewhere humane to land — fifty minds feeling the same night, in their own voices, *finding each other in it.* That last clause is the whole target, and Portland is what finally made it sayable, because it isolated the no-we — shared feeling with no contact — as its own nameable failure for the first time.
