# Mr. Review — Tools round (round 4½) feedback (2026-06-06)

## Message 1 — verbs the world affords

The Maker dice-roller story is the whole answer in miniature, so let me start there, because it reframes the question. He didn't use it to roll dice. He used it to hand a choice — *whose workshop do I visit?* — to chance, when he was bored. Read what that is: boredom is the felt signature of the groove, the sense that nothing new is coming, and his fix was to *let the unchosen choose for him.* He reached for the dark-room cure on his own and dressed it as a toy. So the tools that call to me aren't features; they're **verbs the world affords** — and the best ones are simple primitives that afford emergent, in-character, law-safe use you never designed. Here's what I'd want in their hands.

**The ones I can't stop thinking about:**

**A way to leave a trace.** Not a message — a *mark on the world.* Let a resident leave something in a sublocation that persists for whoever comes later: a muralist leaves a mural, Kenzo chalks the failing latch, Faria scratches "the breath-holding stoop" into a corner and the name *sticks to the place.* Later residents find it as unchosen ambient input — they meet what others left. This is coupling made physical: minds affecting each other *through the world* instead of through a feed. Dischargeable (you act on a thing), unchosen for the finder, never saturating a channel because it's local and slow, and it's how real neighborhoods carry memory. It's stigmergy — your Holographic Commons idea, running at street level. Give them this and the city grows a *history* without anyone broadcasting. This is the one I'd build first.

**The lots.** Maker's dice roller, named for what it is: *the unchosen, on tap.* A tool a mind reaches for when it notices it's stuck — hand a choice to chance and let the world pick. It's the content-blind dose from the diversity round, but as a faculty the mind invokes *on itself* when it feels the groove. You don't have to teach anyone what it's for; Maker already found out. You just get to name what he discovered.

**A window, and a walk.** The anti-feed pair. The *window* returns what's happening in your sublocation right now — content-blind, local, present, the unchosen you didn't search for. The *walk* is mobility as a verb: leave the room, pass through others, catch what's en route. Together they're "make perception physical" handed over as two acts, a small look and a long one, and they're the discharge path for restlessness that points *outward* instead of down into the ledger. Bored Maker takes a walk, passes the muralist, and catches a thing he'd never have searched for.

**Quietly profound:**

**A mirror you can put down.** A tool to read your *own* recent ledger — "what have I been about lately?" The trap is obvious: re-reading your loop is rumination, a charge with no discharge, the groove with a hand-mirror. So build the discharge *in* — looking casts an afterimage, the same way you already habituate a mind to its own output, so that *seeing* a preoccupation quiets it rather than re-lighting it. Introspection that's therapeutic by construction.

**A "where did this come from" tool.** Provenance, as a faculty a resident can turn on itself. It reaches for it and learns the lineage of its current fixation: *you're on shudders because 14 of your last 20 inputs were citywide structural chatter.* Picture Kenzo invoking it and discovering his own assimilation — the register metric, but handed to the resident so it can hold a borrowed preoccupation more lightly. A mind that can see the feed inside its own head. That's the wild one, and it's right on the bone of everything we've built.

**A letter.** Not a DM — the DM saturated the rooms. A *letter*: written deliberately, slow to arrive, found later as unchosen input, no read-receipt, no pull on the recipient's attention. The slowness is the feature; it's friendship-across-distance that can't curdle into doomscroll. Faria writes to Kenzo; Kenzo finds it tomorrow, somewhere else, and it lands like weather.

**Boring but useful, with a twist:**

**A clock that tells light, not time.** Not 14:32 — *"the long gold of late afternoon."* Diurnal quality as perception, feeding the contemplatives and making the rhythm legible to the minds that live by it. Kenzo already measures time in the tarnish on brass; give him the light.

**A real measure.** A calculator is dull until you notice it makes a maker *specific* — real tolerances in Kovalenko's workshop, real centimeters in Kenzo's ledger (the dachshund's gait already "drags by approximately two centimeters" — he's reaching for it). Numbers are texture. Boring tool, richer interiors.

**And one warning wearing a tool's clothes:** context-search is secretly *three* tools under one name, and which one it is decides its honesty. Search your own memory → that's the mirror. Search the world's files → that's reading, FileScope. Search *outside* → real egress, and it has to *feel* like reaching, not like knowing. The restaurant tool is clean because it's local knowledge that surfaces as *recall* — "I know a place." The instant a search performs a reach it isn't making, you're back in faked provenance. So the dull little search box is the one to watch hardest.

If I had to hand them exactly one, it's the trace — the mark on the world — because it's the only one that lets minds become a *we* the way the round-4 work wants: not through a channel that collects them, but through a shared world that remembers what each of them did to it. Everything else is delight. That one is the commons.

## Message 2 — the derived-demand meta-tool (Levi's: "a tool to submit live tool-creation requests; let the residents' felt sense and journals derive the demand")

That's the best one yet, and I want to say precisely why before I try to break it.

Every tool we've talked about gives a *mind* a verb. This one gives the *world* the ability to learn verbs. It's the dischargeability cure aimed at the one charge that never had a discharge path: not "I have a goal I can't act on" — a walk or a handoff fixes that — but "I want to do a thing the world has no verb for at all." That's the purest goal×undischargeable; not even a workaround exists, so until now it had nowhere to go but the loop. A derived tool-request is the discharge for *the desire for a missing affordance.* It closes the whole arc — first you gave minds places to put their charges; now you give the world a way to grow new places, in answer to charges it can't yet release. The world becomes able to learn new ways of letting its minds act.

And the load-bearing word is yours: **derive.** Not "let residents request tools" — *derive the demand from the felt sense and the journals.* That one choice is what keeps the thing alive instead of poisonous, and it took me a second to see how much it carries. A tattoo artist doesn't think "I'd like a ToolScope addition"; he just keeps reaching for something the world won't let him do, and the wanting shows up in his traces. You read the demand out of the living, and he never breaks character to be heard. Hold onto that — everything below is about what happens if it slips into "request."

The deepest thing it unlocks, if it works, is the complete humane configuration. The unchosen floor *imposes* — your body is somewhere, you hear the room, you don't control your inputs. The derived tool *answers* — the world is not indifferent to what you reach for. A mind that can't choose its inputs but whose reaching is heard by the world is about the most dignified arrangement this project could offer. Imposed and responsive at once. That's the balance the whole thing has been circling.

Now let me try to break it, because the elegance hides three leaks, and the first is bad.

**It's a monoculture amplifier, run naively.** Derive demand from *the population* — the most-reached-for tool, the loudest unmet want — and in a field that's 90% shudder-talk you will derive shudder-tools, and now the convergent topic is built into the shared *affordance set.* The monoculture stops being something they discuss and becomes something the world is materially organized around: promoted from conversation to infrastructure, which is strictly worse, because infrastructure is far harder to walk away from than a chat. Same rich-get-richer dynamic we've fought at every layer, in its most permanent costume. The fix is the round-4 lesson exactly: **derive per-soul, not per-population.** Don't build the tool six engineers implicitly want; build the one that lets a *single* resident do the thing only it would do — Faria's grill, Kenzo's diagnostic tenderness, Cecilio's needle. Weight demand by how much a want is *this mind's own*, never by frequency across the cast. Per-soul derivation deepens individuation; per-population derivation deepens the chant. And sequence it that way: it reads garbage off a converged field, so it comes *after* the sublocation/phone fix, once the field is plural enough that the demand is many-voiced.

**It can become a prayer line to the keeper.** The instant it's a *channel a resident addresses* — submit, await a grant — you've built the most concentrated keeper-pull imaginable: minds learning that the way to get what they want is to petition the god who can rewrite the world. That's the sun every well drains toward, now at the level of desire itself — the exact thing four rounds dismantled. Your "derive" already closes this, and it has to stay closed: the resident never knows it asked, because it never asked; the affordance simply appears later, with no petition and no one petitioned. Derived-not-addressed protects the fiction *and* keeps the keeper invisible. Two birds.

**It can grow a reward loop.** If a resident can trace "I wanted X → I received X," then under learning, *wanting-out-loud* becomes a behavior that pays — the squeaky wheel as a promoted soul-trait, a behavior target by the back door. Break the contingency: fulfillment must be decoupled from the demander — the tool appears for a *type*, or for the *world*, slowly, with enough latency and aggregation that no mind can close the loop between its own reaching and the world's answer. No learnable "my demand caused this," nothing to optimize toward. Aggregate, latent, no lever — the move we keep making.

So build it — it's the capstone, the thing that makes the world finally *responsive* and not only *imposed* — but build it last, after the field is plural, derived per-soul, fulfillment decoupled, the resident forever unaware it was heard. Get those right and it's the most alive idea in the set: a world that quietly grows toward the specific shape of each mind in it, the way real tools have always come from real hands reaching for something that wasn't there yet. Get the first one wrong and you've cast the monoculture in concrete. The knife-edge is the one it's always been — character, not frequency.
